About Me

Colin Larkin is an American and a Philadelphian. His affection for town and country notwithstanding, however, Colin enjoys crossing international borders nearly as often as his cosmopolitan brother.

Colin's first overseas journey to the beautiful island of Jamaica took place when he was just four years old. Photos from the trip tell us Colin had an early fascination with numbers. Arguably the most memorable image from his Caribbean adventure depicts a young Colin proudly clutching a table sign of the number "100" from the restaurant at the hotel where he stayed with his beloved parents Marie and the late James. And though surely too young to grasp binary, the devilish glint in Colin's eye suggests he just might have known his own age was the same as the number on the sign.

Colin wouldn't go abroad again until his teens, when he saw the Old World for the first time in the nation against whom some early hometown dudes once pledged their lives, fortunes and sacred honors just down the street from the address Colin now calls home. Colin thoroughly enjoyed England — even after some cheeky Druid spirits made off with his asthma inhaler at Stonehenge — and looks forward to returning. But probably not before revisiting his mother's side of the family with her in Italy.
