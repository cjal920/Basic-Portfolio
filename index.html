About Me

Colin Larkin was born in Philadelphia and is still proud to call it home. And while the city bordering New Jersey to its west may have lost some of its sense of identity and importance as the great island city to its north grew mighty during the last century, Philadelphia, in recent years, has begun to give New York - the metropolis that his admirable younger brother now calls home - a run for its money.

As much as he loves his city and his country, Colin also enjoys to cross international borders as often as his busy schedule permits. His first overseas journey was to Jamaica. And though too young at the time to clearly remember the Caribbean, photos from the trip tell us he had a wonderful time with his mother and father.

Colin wouldn't go abroad again until his teens, when he saw the old world for the first time in the nation whose king once gave his subjects in Philly such a hard time for pledging their lives, fortunes and sacred honor to get the hell away from him. But because he doesn't believe in holding grudges, not to mention the fact that none of his ancestors would even come to America a good century after Jefferson published his Declaration just down the street from his current abode, Colin enjoyed England splendidly (even after losing the inhaler for his asthma at Stonehenge).
